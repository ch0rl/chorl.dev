ACAB

Cops are shit; they won’t wear shoulder numbers, won’t follow PACE, will use excessive force, and won’t like you holding them to account. It is, therefore, important to be informed and stand your ground - as long as you don’t put yourself at unnecessary risk.

Pushing It...

When it comes to standing up to police, knowing how far you can push it is key, as well as your own risk tolerance. Truly, this comes from experience; my advice would be to:

  1. “Read the room”: if things are getting tense, maybe back off a bit.
  2. Watch the back lines: if the police behind the main line start rushing, forming up, or otherwise change what they are doing, keep an eye out for an escalation.
  3. Figure out your risk tolerance beforehand: things can escalate quickly and adrenaline can change your perception of risk. Before going to a protest, I like to talk to a friend about the risks that I would be comfortable taking, to get another person’s thoughts and ensure I understand what I’m getting into. Try to stick to these choices once you’re at the protest, you may have a lesser ability to reason in-the-moment.

The Media

The media, while good for getting coverage of a protest, can be really annoying. Especially where people are trying to use tactics such as de-arrest, forming blockades, or just trying to get bustcards to those arrested, the media can pose a real obstacle. Sadly, I don’t have any advice other than to stand your ground and don’t be afraid to push.

Kit List

These are the things I take to protests (those marked with “†” I take when acting as a liaison/observer):

  • Water – stay hydrated
  • Hi-viz†
  • Snacks (plus ones to share if you can afford it)
  • Bustcards – you can never have too many. I like to put some in little plastic bags, in case it rains
  • Waterproof notepad and pen
  • A protest phone to record / take photos
  • A watch
  • A coat
    • Plasters
    • Wound wipes
    • Tape
    • Shears and scissors
    • Pads
    • Eye wash
    • Foil blanket
    • Triangular bandage
    • Gloves (I like nitrile best)
  • Paracord
  • Tape
  • Tissues, lots of tissues
