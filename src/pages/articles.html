Roberts et al. found that 19% of transfeminine individuals did not continue Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) after four years.a Turban et al. found that 13.1% of transgender people detransitioned.b Of those who detransitioned, Turban et al. found that only 15.9% did so because of at least one internal factor.c

\begin{align} \text{let}\ S &\ \text{be the event that one stops HRT within four years} \\ \text{let}\ R &\ \text{be the event that one truly regrets HRT} \\ p(S|R) &= 1 \\ p(S) &= \frac{0.19 + 0.131}{2} \\ &= 0.1605 \\ p(R|S) &= 0.159 \\ p(R) &= \frac{P(R|S) \cdot P(S)}{P(S|R)} \\ &= \frac{0.159 \cdot 0.1605}{1} \\ &= 0.0255195 \\ &\approx 2.55\%. \end{align}

This would appear to put the true HRT regret rate at 2.55%, of those who start HRT. Comparatively, Wilson, Ronnekleiv-Kelly, and Pawlik found that average patient regret rate is 14.4% – over 5.5 times greater.d

  • [b] Jack L. Turban et al., “Factors Leading to “Detransition” Among Transgender and Gender Diverse People in the United States: A Mixed-Methods Analysis”, LGBT Health, 8/4 (2021), PMID: 33794108, 273–80. doi: 10.1089/lgbt.2020.0437.
